Abdul Sattar Edhi

The man of humanity and compassion

Abdul Sattar Edhi, founder of the Edhi Foundation, dedicated his life to serving humanity regardless of religion, race, or background.

Here's a timeline of Abdul Sattar Edhi's life:

  • 1928 - Born in Bantva, Gujarat (then British India).
  • 1947 - Migrates to Pakistan after partition and witnesses immense suffering, which inspires him to serve humanity.
  • 1951 - Establishes the Edhi Foundation with a small dispensary in Karachi.
  • 1957 - Starts Pakistan’s first free ambulance service, which later becomes the world’s largest volunteer ambulance network.
  • 1970s - Expands services to orphanages, shelters, homes for the elderly, and free hospitals.
  • 1980s - Builds hundreds of welfare centers across Pakistan, offering aid in disasters and crises worldwide.
  • 2000s - Recognized globally as a symbol of charity and compassion.
  • 2016 - Passes away at the age of 88. Laid to rest with full state honors in Karachi.

"No religion is higher than humanity."

-- Abdul Sattar Edhi
